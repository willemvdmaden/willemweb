Curiosity Stoppers in GenAI

A Research Initiative

As generative AI becomes increasingly integrated into design research and practice, we encounter various barriers that often halt deeper engagement before it can begin. These "stoppers" range from oversimplified narratives like "AI is unreliable" or "AI is just theft" to practical implementation challenges and genuine tensions between competing design values.

These barriers often contain legitimate concerns worthy of consideration, but become problematic when they function as conversation-enders that prevent nuanced exploration. Whether someone dismisses AI entirely or adopts it uncritically, both responses can stem from the same oversimplified framings.

This research initiative, emerging from workshops at ACM conferences, aims to move from dead-ends to dialogue. Rather than advocating for or against AI adoption, we help you develop your own informed stance by examining these stoppers more deeply.

The goal isn't to convince anyone to use AI, but to ensure that whatever position you take—embrace, reject, or something in between—is grounded in thoughtful reflection rather than surface-level reactions.

The library shows that we all encounter these challenges, but more importantly, it provides a framework for critical engagement that helps you proceed with caution, whether that means thoughtful integration or informed resistance.

Three Types of Stoppers

Through our research, we've identified three distinct types of barriers that prevent effective GenAI integration:

1. Semantic Stopsigns

Big narratives, catchphrases, or conceptual frameworks that shut down deeper engagement with GenAI before it can begin. These reductive framings provide an illusion of resolution while preventing nuanced exploration.

2. Practical Blockers

Technical or implementation barriers that kill momentum during hands-on GenAI work.

3. Design Tensions

Situations where GenAI creates genuine conflicts between legitimate design goals, values, or priorities—forcing difficult trade-offs between two "good" things.

Research Goals

Through this initiative, we aim to:

  • Document common blockers experienced across different domains and contexts
  • Analyze the root causes and patterns behind these obstacles
  • Develop frameworks and strategies for overcoming blockers
  • Create resources to help others navigate similar challenges
  • Foster a community of practice around thoughtful AI integration
