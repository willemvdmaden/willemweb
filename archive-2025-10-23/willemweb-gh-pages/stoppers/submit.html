Share Your Experience

Your experience with curiosity stoppers in generative AI helps us understand how these technologies interact with real-world practice. Whether you've found solutions or you're still stuck, we'd love to learn from your insights.

💡 "I'm stuck" responses are incredibly valuable!

Don't worry if you haven't found solutions yet. These honest representations of where people actually are can help others feel less alone in their confusion and create opportunities for the community to offer suggestions.

Category Examples

To help you choose the right category, here are examples of blockers from each type:

Semantic Stopsigns

Big narratives, catchphrases, or conceptual frameworks that shut down deeper engagement with GenAI before it can begin.

Political

Institutional policies or power dynamics that prevent GenAI exploration

Social

Cultural attitudes or peer pressure that discourage GenAI engagement

Conceptual

Oversimplified beliefs about GenAI that shut down deeper thinking

Practical Blockers

Technical or implementation barriers that kill momentum during hands-on GenAI work.

Technical Limitations

The technology simply can't do what you need it to do

Workflow Disruption

GenAI creates more work or confusion than it solves

Skill Gaps

Missing knowledge or training needed to use GenAI effectively

Resource Constraints

Lack of time, budget, or computational resources to implement properly

Design Tensions

Situations where GenAI creates genuine conflicts between legitimate design goals, values, or priorities.

Experience vs. Control

AI enhances user experience but reduces designer control over outcomes

Efficiency vs. Quality

AI speeds up processes but potentially compromises depth or craftsmanship

Innovation vs. Tradition

AI enables new possibilities but conflicts with established practices or values

Accessibility vs. Authenticity

AI makes things more accessible but may dilute original intent or meaning

Personalization vs. Privacy

AI customization requires data trade-offs that feel uncomfortable

Automation vs. Agency

AI handles tasks well but removes human agency from meaningful processes

Privacy and Next Steps

What Happens Next

  • • You'll receive a confirmation email with your submission details
  • • We'll review your contribution and may reach out for clarification
  • • Once approved, your experience will appear in the public library
  • • You'll be invited to participate in collaborative analysis sessions (optional)
  • • You'll have the opportunity to co-author any resulting publications

Privacy Protection

  • • You can choose to be identified or remain anonymous
  • • Organization and project details can be anonymized
  • • You control how your contribution is used in publications
  • • You can withdraw your participation at any time
  • • All data is stored securely according to GDPR guidelines
